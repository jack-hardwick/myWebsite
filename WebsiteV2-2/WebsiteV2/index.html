From Russia with Maps

I was born in Russia but was lucky enough to become a naturalized US citizen. Since then I've been all over the place.
















































Nothing without Providence

Colorado has been my home since 2008. It is also where my career with computer science and GIS began.
















































Longmont, Colorado

While my experience with GIS began at Pike's Peak Community College in Colorado Springs, the interesting portion of my journey began in Longmont, Colorado. Starting June 2017, I worked as a Senior Help Desk Technician for the FRCC IT Department and later as a GIS Intern for the City of Longmont. In May 2018, I earned an Associate's of Applied Science in Geospatial Science at Front Range Community College in Longmont.
















































Colorado Springs, Colorado

After my time at FRCC, I caught the GIS bug. I needed to learn more and pursue a 4 year degree. I set my sights back at my home in Colorado Springs. Specifically, the University of Colorado Colorado Springs
















































UCCS

My time at FRCC had taught me a lot about applying GIS in a practical sense but not given me a lot of real world opportunities to practice those skills. That all changed at UCCS. Shortly after arriving, I was hired onto the Office of Information Technology Help Desk team as a Student Technician and as a Teaching Assistant for the Geography and Environmental Studies Department.
















































GIS Technician

A few months into my employment with OIT, I had a discussion with our Network Analyst Bobby Baker. We talked about authoratitive information and the lack of it in regards to the fiber infrastructure across campus. A few weeks later, I submitted a proposal to the CIO and Vice Chancellor of Technology, Harper Johnson, arguing that GIS would be able to fufill this need and the steps needed to do so. In November of 2018, I was hired on as a GIS Technician for the OIT Department and have been creating GIS products for UCCS ever since.
